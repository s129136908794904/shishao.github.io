Data Science & Atmospheric Chemistry

Hello, I'm Shao Shi.

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Southern University of Science and Technology developing data mining algorithms and single-particle measurement techniques to unravel the chemistry of atmospheric aerosols.

My work bridges environmental science with machine learning—crafting unsupervised clustering methods, field measurement workflows, and ensemble models to turn large-scale atmospheric datasets into actionable insights.

Research Interests

I seek to advance data-centric approaches for atmospheric sciences, with emphasis on designing unsupervised clustering algorithms, signal processing pipelines, and ensemble learning frameworks tailored to high-volume mass spectrometric and aerosol datasets.

I am open to interdisciplinary collaborations that couple environmental measurements with domain-agnostic data mining, whether in chemical, biological, or physical systems.

Research Experience

I. Atmospheric Single-Particle Measurement

Developing standard-free mass calibration and quantitative analysis workflows that unlock rich chemical characterization of ambient aerosols.

  • Standard-free calibration for single-particle mass spectrometry (Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics, 2024).
  • Entropy-controlled continuous wavelet transform for precise peak extraction (in preparation).

II. Data Mining at Atmospheric Scale

Designing unsupervised algorithms and similarity measures that keep pace with ever-growing aerosol datasets.

  • Granted Chinese patent on incremental clustering of aerosol mass spectra.
  • FASC: Flexible Adaptive Stable Clustering algorithm for mass spectrometric big data (in preparation).

III. Ambient Aerosol Monitoring

Linking measurements to health and climate through targeted field campaigns and ensemble learning models.

  • Ship emission impacts on air quality under new marine fuel regulations (Environmental Science & Technology, 2023).
  • Respiratory deposition and health effects of ultrafine particles; hygroscopicity-composition linkages (multiple works, 2023–2024).
